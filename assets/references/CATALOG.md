# Reference image catalog

300 reference images + 1 video (`019_VID_20190903_001445.mp4`), pulled from a Google Photos
inspiration album and classified by vision agents. Each row: what the image shows and the concrete
design idea it suggests for a house designed from first principles.

**Status:** working set, currently *untracked* (not committed). See "Privacy & cleanup" before
publishing any of this to the public repo.

---

## Synthesis — what the album keeps arguing for

The 300 images aren't random; the same handful of convictions recur dozens of times. Distilled:

1. **Small footprint, big vertical volume.** Sleeping lofts/mezzanines over living space, vaulted
   timber ceilings rising to a glazed gable, double-height voids with glass balustrades. The album
   repeatedly makes a tiny plan feel large by going *up*, not *out* (138, 224, 227, 269, 287, 011, 260).

2. **Dissolve the wall toward the view.** Full-height and corner glazing, sliding/bi-fold glass walls,
   frameless folding terrace enclosures — the best wall on the view side is barely a wall (010, 027,
   060, 068, 082, 098, 198, 030). Pair with adjustable shading (sheer verticals, roller shades,
   motorized dual-layer sheer+blackout) because all that glass needs glare control (051, 119, 128,
   240, 275, 280).

3. **Passive-solar first, mechanical second.** Sun-angle sketches, roof-overhang sizing (winter sun in,
   summer sun out), water-column thermal mass, and a *complete continental set of Köppen climate maps* —
   the design wants to be tuned to its exact climate zone before any HVAC is specified (104, 155, 159,
   176, 199, 200, 229, 261, 285, 196). Then: HRV/MVHR heat recovery and ground-source heat pumps as the
   mechanical layer (012, 031, 073, 217, 246, 254).

4. **Energy independence baked in from day one.** Rooftop PV + solar-thermal, Tesla Solar Roof
   (generation *in* the tiles), home battery bank, EV/carport charging — treated as design inputs, not
   add-ons (206, 296, 087, 038, 009).

5. **Workshop as a first-class room.** Dozens of images: French-cleat and pegboard/OSB tool walls,
   labeled small-parts bin grids, fold-up workbenches, lumber racks. A maker/fab space is clearly
   non-negotiable in this house (052, 105, 135, 137, 250, 281, 288, 293, 298, 299, 008, 034, 036).

6. **One modular storage system, see-through and stackable.** IKEA SAMLA clear bins and BrickBox open
   cubes appear again and again — the principle is *standardize on one box family in graduated sizes* and
   design shelf depths around it; cubes double as room dividers (042, 047, 089, 160, 194, 219, 223, 253,
   271, 256 finger-pull detail).

7. **Wet-zones consolidated and low-tech.** Curbless wet-room shower trays with linear drains, wall-hung
   toilets with integrated bidet jets, cheap mechanical bidet attachments, composting toilet under a
   plywood bench — plumbing grouped along one wall to shorten runs (097, 114, 130, 172, 108, 265, 061, 136).

8. **Prefab / modular delivery.** Several show a complete house module craned or trucked onto a site-built
   stone plinth — build off-site, set on-site (233, 273, 257, 123, 295, 009 containers).

9. **Three-legged stools, everywhere.** A running motif — tripod bases sit stable on uneven floors; the
   album treats simple solid-wood joinery as the furniture-quality benchmark (029, 048, 064, 096, 131,
   132, 141, 192, 197, 216).

10. **Design verified against the human body.** Multiple 3D layouts draw a person's turning/reach circle
    at appliances, doorways and in the bathroom — clearances checked before building (037, 083, 134, 146,
    282).

11. **First-principles extreme:** a Mars-habitat competition poster (276) — sealed dome, central green
    courtyard, zoned pods. The literal "design a dwelling from scratch" reference.

**Gaps worth filling next:** the album is thin on structural systems/foundations, real cost/BOM numbers,
fire egress, acoustic separation, and water/septic for an off-grid site. Good candidates for the next
reference pull.
